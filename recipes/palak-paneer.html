Palak Paneer

Description

Palak Paneer, a beloved vegetarian dish from the heart of Indian cuisine, is a symphony of flavors and textures that will tantalize your taste buds. This classic recipe combines the delicate creaminess of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) with the vibrant green goodness of spinach, resulting in a dish that's not only delicious but also highly nutritious.

The preparation of Palak Paneer begins with fresh spinach leaves that are blanched, retaining their vibrant green color and earthy essence. These tender greens are then transformed into a lush puree, creating the luscious, verdant base for the dish.

Cubes of paneer, lightly fried to a golden hue, are gently simmered in a fragrant mixture of tomatoes, onions, and a medley of aromatic spices. The marriage of these ingredients brings forth a harmonious blend of flavors that is both comforting and invigorating.

Ingredients:

  • Fresh spinach leaves
  • Paneer (Indian cottage cheese)
  • Tomatoes
  • Onions
  • Spices:
    • Garam masala
    • Turmeric powder
    • Cumin seeds
    • Coriander powder
    • Red chili powder
    • Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Oil or ghee for cooking
  • Fresh cream (optional, for garnish)

    • Steps

    1. Wash and Blanch the Spinach:
      • Thoroughly wash fresh spinach leaves.
      • Boil a pot of water and blanch the spinach leaves for 2-3 minutes until wilted.
      • Transfer the blanched spinach to a bowl of ice water to retain its vibrant green color.
      • Drain and blend the spinach into a smooth puree.
    2. Fry the Paneer:
      • Cut paneer into cubes or slices.
      • Heat oil or ghee in a pan and lightly fry the paneer until it turns golden brown. Set it aside.
    3. Prepare the Spice Mixture:
      • Heat oil or ghee in a pan.
      • Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle.
      • Add finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
      • Add spices: garam masala, turmeric powder, coriander powder, and red chili powder. Sauté briefly.
    4. Add Tomato and Spinach Puree:
      • Add finely chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and the oil separates.
      • Pour in the spinach puree and stir well.
    5. Simmer and Add Paneer:
      • Simmer the mixture for a few minutes until the flavors meld together.
      • Add the fried paneer pieces and kasuri methi. Simmer for a few more minutes.
    6. Garnish and Serve:
      • Garnish with a drizzle of fresh cream and a pinch of garam masala.
      • Serve Palak Paneer hot with naan, roti, or rice.
