Butter Roti

Description

Butter Roti, also known as "Butter Naan," is a quintessential Indian flatbread that holds a special place in the hearts and palates of people around the world. This soft, pillowy bread, enriched with the richness of butter, is a comforting and versatile staple in Indian cuisine.

The preparation of Butter Roti begins with a simple mixture of whole wheat flour, water, and a pinch of salt. The dough is kneaded to perfection, resulting in a smooth and elastic texture that allows for effortless rolling and cooking. Each portion of the dough is skillfully shaped into round discs, and then comes the magic â€“ butter.

Once on the hot griddle, these rotis are lightly coated with glistening butter, which not only enhances the flavor but also gives them a beautiful golden sheen. The aroma of cooking Butter Roti wafts through the air, promising a delightful meal to come.

Ingredients

  • Whole wheat flour
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Butter

Steps

  1. Mix Dough:
    • In a bowl, combine whole wheat flour and a pinch of salt.
    • Gradually add water and knead to form a smooth and soft dough.
  2. Divide into Balls:
    • Divide the dough into small, equal-sized balls.
  3. Roll Out the Roti:
    • Take a dough ball and flatten it with your hands to form a small disc.
    • Using a rolling pin, roll out the dough into a thin, round roti.
  4. Cook Roti:
    • Heat a flat griddle (tava) on medium-high heat.
    • Place the rolled-out roti on the hot griddle.
    • Cook until you see bubbles forming on the surface.
    • Flip the roti and cook the other side until it puffs up and has golden brown spots.
  5. Apply Butter:
    • Remove the roti from the griddle and immediately apply a generous amount of butter on one side.
  6. Serve:
    • Serve the delicious Butter Roti hot with your favorite curry or side dish.
