Dal Tadka

Description:

Dal Tadka, a popular Indian dish, is a flavorful and comforting lentil stew made with yellow lentils tempered with aromatic spices. This dish is known for its rich, creamy texture and the tantalizing "tadka" (tempering) of ghee, cumin, and red chili powder that is drizzled on top, adding an extra layer of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • Yellow lentils (Toor or Arhar dal)
  • Water
  • Turmeric powder
  • Salt
  • Tomatoes
  • Green chilies
  • Ghee (clarified butter) or oil
  • Cumin seeds
  • Asafoetida (hing)
  • Red chili powder
  • Coriander leaves (cilantro) for garnish

Steps to Make Dal Tadka:

  1. Wash and Soak Lentils:
    • Wash the yellow lentils thoroughly.
    • Soak the lentils in water for about 30 minutes.
  2. Cook Lentils:
    • Drain the soaked lentils and place them in a large pot.
    • Add water, turmeric powder, and salt.
    • Cook the lentils until they are soft and mushy.
  3. Prepare Tadka (Tempering):
    • In a separate pan, heat ghee or oil.
    • Add cumin seeds and asafoetida (hing).
    • Add chopped green chilies and saut√© briefly.
    • Now, add red chili powder and tomatoes. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil separates.
  4. Combine Tadka with Dal:
    • Add the prepared tadka to the cooked lentils and mix well.
    • Simmer the dal for a few minutes to blend the flavors.
  5. Garnish and Serve:
    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves (cilantro).
    • Serve hot with rice or Indian bread, such as roti or naan.
